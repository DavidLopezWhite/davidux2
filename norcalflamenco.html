NorCal Flamenco

  • My Roles:Visual and UX Design, Wordpress Admin.
  • Client:Personal Project
  • Platform:Web and Mobile
  • Timeline:4 months
  • Year:2014-present
creating a collaborative space for flamenco events

There is a sizeable flamenco community in the central coast of California, with artists, audiences and students. Searching for "flamenco San Francisco" reveals limited results, though I know there are other shows etc. out there. Let's build them a place where it's easy to find out about what's happening!

My Role
  • Stakeholder interviews.
  • Usability testing.
  • Site navigation, information architecture and user flows.
  • Artist and regular event pages.
  • Continued curation of website.
  • Tracking Google Analytics.
Community

I wanted to provide a central location where folks could find out about flamenco related events. There had been a previous site in our area for this purpose, but it was deactivated a few years before and its passing left a void.



Preliminary interviews showed support and enthusiasm for the project. What I found out, was that my idea of the community was very different than the reality.

Visit Site
 
Feedback from artists and community members informed my process. Over time, I came to understand that it would require more than just a big idea to bring people together, and make this project a success.

Solution

I conducted interviews with stakeholders, artists, event producers and active students and concertgoers during the design and build of the site.

What I learned was that people did want the website, but were unwilling or unable to do the work to take the step to create thier promotional materials on another platform.

  • Artists have to post on multiple channels for each event. Adding one more is a deal-breaker.
  • For many folks, accessing events via Facebook is the most convenient way to keep track of thier social media schedule.
  • If contect creation is up to one person, it defeats the purpose of a community site, events calendar and archive.
