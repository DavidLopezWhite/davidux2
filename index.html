About Me

Years of using online tutorials gave me an appreciative eye for how design impacts learning, and I began studying Instructional Design, which led me to Human Centered Design.

Understanding and engaging with how people learn has been a longtime fascination, that drives my passion for human-centered design.

Connecting with people of diverse backgrounds and with different communication styles has shown me that empathy and listening are crucial for relationships to form and grow. This philosophy is a cornerstone of my value set as a designer.

What I also love about design is that it has a process, making it like a science. But because the world is wobbly, the design process is also flexible. As a way to identify problems and solve them, this powerful set of tools is something that can be applied to any aspect of life - and that is truly inspirational.

I am an armchair historian with a soft spot for plants and conversations about comparative philosophy. And I'm a proud dad!
